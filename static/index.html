The Problem

Why FCFS Fails 314 Million Connections

Every Indian LPG distributor uses First-Come, First-Served. It sounds fair. It isn't.

No Priority Differentiation

A hotel ordering 10 cylinders is processed before a household needing 1 — simply because it booked earlier. Families go without cooking gas.

All-or-Nothing Blocking

3 cylinders left, next order needs 5? FCFS skips it. Those 3 cylinders sit idle in the warehouse while consumers wait.

Zero Crisis Response

During Karnataka's 2023 floods, 50,000+ families displaced. FCFS had no mechanism to freeze commercial orders or protect households.

Our Solution

Four Innovations. Same Complexity.

The Smart Priority Engine runs at O(n log n) — identical to FCFS — but with fundamentally better outcomes.

Three-Tier Priority Classification

Orders sorted by consumer class before processing. Households always first, aligned with PMUY and NFSA mandates.

Domestic — Rank 1
Essential — Rank 2
Commercial — Rank 3

Pro-Rata Splitting

If we can't fill the full order, deliver what's available and auto-backorder the rest. 3 left, need 5? Deliver 3, track the remaining 2.

Zero Warehouse Waste

Anti-Starvation Ageing

Priority score drops 0.02/day. Commercial reaches domestic-level priority in a bounded maximum of 100 days. Provably fair.

Mathematically Guaranteed

One-Click Crisis Mode

During floods or supply disruptions, administrators freeze commercial allocation instantly. Domestic consumers get protected supply with configurable rationing limits. Emergency response in seconds.
